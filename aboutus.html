About Us

This page celebrates the life and groundbreaking contributions of some of the most influential scientists of the 20th century: Albert Einstein, Marie Curie, J.J. Thomson, DuPont's Crawford, Enrico Fermi, and Robert Oppenheimer.

Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein is best known for his theory of relativity, fundamentally changing our understanding of space and time.

"Imagination is more important than knowledge." – Albert Einstein

Marie Curie

Marie Curie, a pioneer in radioactivity, was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and remains the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields.

"Nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood." – Marie Curie

J.J. Thomson

J.J. Thomson discovered the electron, laying the foundation for much of modern physics.

"The electron is the key to the atom." – J.J. Thomson

DuPont's Crawford

Herbert C. Crawford was pivotal in developing materials like synthetic rubber, impacting industrial chemistry.

"Innovation is the ability to see change as an opportunity." – Herbert Crawford

Enrico Fermi

Enrico Fermi's work on nuclear reactions led to the creation of the first nuclear reactor.

"If I could explain it to the average person, I wouldn’t have been worth the Nobel Prize." – Enrico Fermi

Robert Oppenheimer

Robert Oppenheimer is known for leading the Manhattan Project, which developed the first nuclear weapons.

"Now I am become Death, the destroyer of worlds." – Robert Oppenheimer
