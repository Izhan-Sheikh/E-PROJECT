About Marie Curie

Marie Curie (1867–1934) was a Polish-French physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and remains the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences: Physics (1903) and Chemistry (1911).

Her groundbreaking work included the discovery of two radioactive elements, polonium and radium. Marie Curie's dedication to science helped lay the foundations for modern medical treatments, including the use of X-rays in diagnostics.

Despite facing significant challenges as a woman in science during her time, Curie’s relentless pursuit of knowledge and her contributions to humanity remain an inspiration to generations of scientists.
