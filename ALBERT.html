About Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein (1879-1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist who is best known for developing the theory of relativity. His theory of relativity, which includes the famous equation E=mcÂ², fundamentally changed our understanding of space, time, and energy.

Beyond his scientific achievements, Einstein was a passionate advocate for civil rights, peace, and humanitarian causes. He publicly opposed nationalism and advocated for global cooperation. His moral and political views reflected his deep concern for the welfare of humanity, making him not only a brilliant scientist but also a respected global figure.

Einstein's work on the photoelectric effect, which earned him the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921, was pivotal in the development of quantum theory. His revolutionary ideas in both physics and philosophy continue to shape modern science to this day.
